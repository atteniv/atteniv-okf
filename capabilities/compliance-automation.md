**Strategic Pillar II.** Turns [presence data](presence-detection.md) into
policy enforcement and progressive discipline — without invasive tracking or
manager firefighting.

# What It Does

Automates the entire attendance disciplinary lifecycle — from first violation
through termination review — with full documentation at every step. Managers get
back the ~40% of administrative time otherwise spent on attendance tracking,
follow-up, and documentation.

# How It Works

Configurable violation thresholds trigger automatic
[progressive discipline](../concepts/progressive-discipline.md) escalation
(default policy shown; the business-rules engine is fully configurable per
company):

| Violations | Action | Notification |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| 4 | Verbal warning | Manager notified, 5-day response window |
| 6 | Written warning | HR notified, documentation required |
| 8 | Final warning | — |
| 9 | Termination review | — |

The system auto-generates templated warning letters at each stage and tracks
employee acknowledgment of each warning.

Supporting features (from the platform's Infractions surface): escalation status
across the configurable policy (Verbal → Written → Final → Termination),
threshold tracking, escalation progress bars, and CSV export for HR review.

# Exception & Accommodation Management (Critical Differentiator)

The enforcement engine manages three accommodation types **natively — before any
action is triggered.** No other attendance platform manages these exceptions
within the enforcement engine itself; this is the primary ADA and FMLA risk
differentiator.

1. **Pre-return scheduling** — Phased return plans and modified hours after a
   leave of absence. The violation counter does not begin until the modified
   schedule is satisfied.
2. **Ongoing ADA accommodations** — Permanent hybrid exceptions for employees
   with disabilities. Tracked without expiration until formally removed. Never
   trigger escalation.
3. **Intermittent FMLA** — Protected variable absences. Excluded from violation
   counting even without a predictable absence schedule.

> The enforcement engine knows who the exceptions are *before* it acts, and the
> audit trail is built for legal defensibility.
